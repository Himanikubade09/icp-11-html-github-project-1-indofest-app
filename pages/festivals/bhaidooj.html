BHAI-DOOJ :

About Bhai-Dooj:

Bhai Dooj is a Hindu festival that celebrates the loving relationship between brothers and sisters. It is celebrated on the fifth day of Diwali (the new moon day or Bhai Dooj day), which usually falls two days after Diwali. It is a day for brothers to visit their sisters and for sisters to bless their brothers with long life, prosperity, and happiness. Bhai Dooj is a beautiful celebration of the unbreakable bond between brothers and sisters. It's a day of sharing love, protecting each other, and thanking each other for all the support throughout life. Whether you celebrate it in a traditional manner or with modern touches, the festival brings families closer and strengthens relationships.

Best place to visit:

1.VARANASI, UTTAR PRADESH – Spiritual & Festive Experience 🕉️

Varanasi is known for its spiritual significance, and during festivals like Bhai Dooj, it comes alive with rituals and prayers. Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is one of the most beautiful experiences, and you can celebrate the festival with your sibling at the ghats, offering prayers for prosperity and good health. Many families gather by the river to mark the festival and exchange blessings. Shopping for traditional souvenirs like brassware, religious artifacts, and sweets is also a part of the experience.
📍 Must-Visit Places:

  • Dashashwamedh Ghat
  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple
  • Sankat Mochan Temple



2. Jaipur, Rajasthan – Royal Vibes with Sibling Celebrations 👑

Bhai Dooj celebrations in Jaipur have a royal charm due to the city's rich cultural heritage. You can enjoy traditional meals with your sibling in a grand setting. Jaipur also hosts kites festivals around this time, adding a unique festive flair to the day. Amber Fort, Hawa Mahal, and City Palace offer splendid backdrops for a family outing. Rajasthani sweets and meals are a must-try here.
📍 Must-Visit Places:

  • Amber Fort
  • Hawa Mahal
  • City Palace
  • Jantar Mantar

How to visit VARANASI:

1.By Air (Fastest Option) ✈️

Nearest Airport: Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport (VNS), about 25 km from the city center.
  • Domestic Flights: Varanasi has direct flights to/from major cities such as Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Lucknow, Bengaluru, and Chennai.
  • International Flights: There are limited international connections (like from Bangladesh, Thailand, UAE, and Nepal), but most international travelers connect through Delhi or Mumbai.

🚖 From Airport to City Center (Park Street/Howrah/Esplanade):
  • Prepaid taxis (₹300-500) or app-based cabs (Uber/Ola).
  • Alternatively, you can opt for local auto-rickshaws or private cabs for a more affordable option.



2.By Train 🚆 (Affordable & Convenient) VARANASI has two major railway stations:
  1. Howrah Junction (HWH) – Largest station, well-connected to Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, and other major cities.
  2. Sealdah Junction (SDAH) – Best for trains from Northeast India & Bihar.
🚖 From Railway Station to City Center:
  • Yellow taxis, app-based cabs, buses, and Metro available.
