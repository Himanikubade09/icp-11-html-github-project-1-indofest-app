Lohri

About Lohri:

Lohri is a popular festival celebrated primarily in the northern regions of India, especially in Punjab, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, and Jammu. It marks the end of winter and the beginning of the harvest season, particularly for crops like sugarcane, sesame, and mustard. It is celebrated on the 13th of January every year, marking the end of the winter solstice.A central feature of the festival is the lighting of a bonfire, around which people gather to sing traditional songs, dance (especially Bhangra and Gidda), and throw offerings like sesame seeds, sugarcane, and jaggery into the fire as a symbol of gratitude for the harvest.People enjoy foods like til (sesame seeds), jaggery, rewri, and popcorn, which are believed to bring good health and prosperity.Lohri is not only about the harvest but also celebrates the communityâ€™s togetherness, the warmth of the bonfire, and the joy of new beginnings.

Best place to visit:

  1. Amritsar, Punjab

    2. Amritsar is the cultural and spiritual heart of Punjab, where the celebration of Lohri is deeply rooted. The Golden Temple adds a serene touch to the lively festivities, making it a unique and unforgettable destination. Participate in the grand bonfire celebrations, where locals gather to sing traditional songs and dance to Bhangra music. Enjoy festive treats like til, gajak, and rewri, which are shared among family and friends. You can also enjoy cultural performances and rituals associated with Lohri.During Lohri, local cultural programs are organized, including kavi sammelans (poetry sessions), pujas, and other events that celebrate the harvest and the significance of fire in Punjabi tradition.

  2. Patiala, Punjab

    3. Known for its rich royal heritage, Patiala celebrates Lohri in a grand manner. The festivities here are full of energy, with vibrant folk music, dance, and rituals that reflect the cultural richness of Punjab.Join the lively processions, watch traditional Bhangra and Gidda performances, and enjoy traditional Lohri sweets like til and rewri. The overall atmosphere of Patiala during Lohri is one of joy and celebration.Patiala is one of the best places to experience the true spirit of Lohri. The celebrations here are full of life, with families, friends, and even strangers coming together to celebrate the harvest, bid farewell to winter, and welcome the warmth of the new season. Whether you're in the city center or a local village, the infectious energy and the beautiful sights of bonfires and dancing make Patiala a perfect destination for Lohri.
